Agentic AI Lab: From Healthcare Insurance Advocacy to Personal AI Agents

MIS 432 · AI in Business · Western Washington University



In this lab, I built AI agent workflows using n8n and large language models. I created an AI Insurance Advocate that analyzes denied claims, pulls patient charts, checks insurance policy rules, and drafts appeal letters with evidence. I also built a personal agent for event planning. This project showed me how powerful agentic AI becomes when it is connected to real tools and governed properly.

1. What is Agentic AI?

Agentic AI goes beyond chatbots by using tools and making decisions about what steps to take next. Instead of responding once, it runs a loop of observing, thinking, acting, and checking results.

Reflection

A Level 2 system would require step-by-step instructions, while a Level 3 agentic system can independently plan and execute tasks like managing group projects or schedules. I would still want human approval for final decisions.

2. AI Insurance Advocate

I built an AI Insurance Advocate in n8n that reads denial letters, pulls patient medical records, checks insurance policy rules, and drafts appeal letters.

SYSTEM PROMPT: You are an insurance appeal specialist. Use patient chart + policy data to draft appeal letters. Do not hallucinate or fabricate information. Only use verified facts from tools.

Reflection

The agent followed the full observe → think → act → check workflow and decided tool usage on its own, which made it feel like a real autonomous system.

3. Second Agent — Concert Planner

I also built a personal agent that recommends concerts based on schedule, budget, and events available.

Reflection

This agent uses tools for events, calendar, and budget to recommend the best option instead of requiring manual step-by-step input.

4. Auditing & Governance

I evaluated risks such as hallucinations, missing policy data, and errors at scale. Even accurate systems still require human oversight.

Reflection

Governance is essential in healthcare AI. Without proper checks, even small error rates can impact many patients.

5. Ethics & Risk

The biggest concern is errors at scale. Even a small failure rate becomes significant when processing hundreds of cases.

Reflection

I support using AI agents with strict human-in-the-loop approval and automated verification before any real-world action.

6. Personal Agent Reflection

My personal agent is helpful but not yet fully trustworthy for unsupervised use.

Reflection

I would require long-term testing, logs, and accuracy tracking before giving full autonomy.

Key Takeaways

  • Agentic AI becomes powerful when connected to tools and workflows.
  • Human oversight is still necessary for high-stakes decisions.
  • AI systems must be audited for accuracy, not just performance.
  • Governance is as important as model quality.
